⚛️ Physics – Important Questions (2023–25)

Q1. State Newton’s three laws of motion with examples.
1. **1st law (Inertia):** A body remains at rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by external force.
Example: Book on table stays until pushed.
2. **2nd law (F=ma):** Acceleration ∝ force, a = F/m.
Example: Pushing cart gives acceleration.
3. **3rd law:** Every action has equal & opposite reaction.
Example: Gun recoils when fired.
Q2. Derive equation of motion: s = ut + ½at²
v = u + at → v - u = at
s = average velocity × time = (u+v)/2 × t = (u + (u+at))/2 × t = ut + ½ at²
Q3. Work-energy theorem.
Work done by net force = change in kinetic energy: W = ΔKE = ½mv² - ½mu²
Example: Pushing car increases its kinetic energy.
Q4. State law of universal gravitation and derive gravitational field.
F = G m₁m₂/r²
Gravitational field at point = g = F/m = GM/r²
Q5. Elastic collision – definition and example.
Collision where kinetic energy is conserved.
Example: Two billiard balls collide elastically.
Q6. Define momentum and impulse. Relation between them.
Momentum: p = mv
Impulse: J = FΔt
Relation: J = Δp
Q7. Derive expression for power in terms of force and velocity.
P = Work/time = F·s / t = F·v
Q8. Simple harmonic motion – equation and energy.
x = A sin(ωt + φ)
Total energy E = ½ kA² = KE + PE
Q9. Define centripetal force & acceleration. Give formula.
Fc = mv²/r
Ac = v²/r (towards center)
Q10. Simple numerical on projectile motion.
Horizontal range R = u² sin2θ / g
Maximum height H = u² sin²θ / 2g
Tip: Practice 1–2 examples for speed in exam.
Q11. Explain work done by variable force (integration method).
W = ∫ F dx
Example: W = ∫ kx dx = ½ kx² (spring)
Q12. Gravitational potential energy and escape velocity.
U = -GMm/r
Escape velocity v = √(2GM/R)
Q13. Kepler’s laws of planetary motion.
1. Orbit of planets is ellipse with sun at focus.
2. Line joining planet & sun sweeps equal area in equal time.
3. T² ∝ r³
Q14. Define impulse-momentum theorem with example.
Impulse = change in momentum: J = Δp
Example: Catching a ball reduces momentum over longer Δt to reduce force.
Q15. Oscillations – frequency, period, angular frequency.
ω = 2πf = 2π/T
T = period, f = frequency
Q16. Laws of motion in non-inertial frame (pseudo forces).
F' = ma + F_pseudo
Example: Accelerating lift, outward push in circular motion.
Q17. Work done in moving charge in electric field.
W = qΔV
Tip: Practice small numerical for exam.
Q18. Oscillatory motion – energy of simple harmonic oscillator.
KE = ½ mω²(A² - x²), PE = ½ k x², E_total = KE + PE = constant
Q19. Derive expression for velocity of sound in air using Newton’s formula.
v = √(P/ρ)
P = pressure, ρ = density of medium
Q20. Simple numerical on combination of oscillations / velocity in 2D motion.
Use vector components: Rx = x-component, Ry = y-component
Tip: Draw diagram, apply Pythagoras for resultant.
